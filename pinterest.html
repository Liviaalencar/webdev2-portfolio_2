Pinterest

Introduction

Pinterest is a well-known established company which is a picture-based website/app. It inspires people in different ways, fashion, art,recipes, design, DIY etc.

It launched in Marched 2010, the founders are Ben Silbermann, Pual Sciarra and Evan Sharp and the CEO is Ben Silbermann and the number of empoyees with 1,600.

If you like an image/idea , when you click into the post you can save it by clicking the red button called "pin" at the top right corner and they also give you a link below the image to their website so you can find more information on it.

Registration

Its very easy to sign up to Pinterest, you can use it by connecting it to your facebook or even just sign up by using your email.

Pinterest is available on android phones, iphones, windows computers and macs.

You can login with your email, google or Facebook. You must be over the age of 13 to join, just like a lot other of applications.

All you need to do is add your name, gender and what your interest are, e.g.: fashion, art, wedding dresses, ... ; based on your choices images will come up for you on the homepage.

How To Use

Once you enter the website you wil see different images na das you scroll down, more images will be displayed.

On the top of the page you will have the navigation bar. Looking at it from the left to the right you will see the search bar, the home button, the following button, your profile image, a conversation balloon with three dots inside it and, finally, a bell icon (for the notifications).

Once you find picture you like you can save it to your pins. Pins act like your recent favorites and can be accessed whenever you want. You can pin it to a board which is the equivalent to an album, they can be named like you want. When you want to see you pins you saved you ust need to go to you profile.

Cautions and Warnings

When searching about the cautions about the app I couldnt really fidn any apart from articles about Plagirism and copyright claims.People often "pin" images that are linked directly to the site and although a lot of artist are ok with this some are not. With a lot of them clamping down on the unlicensed use of their image. It also doesnt help that you Pinterest account is your full name and can be linked to you Google or Facebook account making it easier for them to find out who you are. But its very rare that the copyright claim is given to the users. Pinterest is more likely to face Copyright claims than its users.

Pinterest is an app for ages 12+ and can sometimes you can find content on the app that could be triggering. Pinterest moderates all of its content but when moderating ceratin words they can't locate all of it. One word they recetly moderated was "anti-vaccanation" but were only able to clear up 88 percent of that content meaning that some of it was still on the app. When somone flags a post its removes the post three times faster than if the post was found by Pinterest themselves so when coming across content that youd dont enjoy or like its best to flag it.
